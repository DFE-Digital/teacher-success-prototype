Professional Conduct

{% include "prepare/sidebar.html" %}

As a teacher trainee, you’re stepping into a professional environment where your behaviour, attitude and approach matter. You’re not only representing yourself, but also your training provider and the wider teaching profession. Demonstrating professional conduct helps build trust with pupils, colleagues and mentors—and sets the tone for your future career.

What does professional conduct look like?

Professional conduct includes how you present yourself, how you communicate, and how you respond to challenges. You should aim to:

  • be punctual and reliable
  • dress appropriately for your school’s setting
  • communicate respectfully with pupils, staff and parents
  • maintain confidentiality and follow safeguarding procedures
  • be reflective and open to feedback
  • uphold the values of your school and the teaching profession

Respecting boundaries

You’ll be working closely with pupils and staff, so it’s important to understand and respect professional boundaries. This includes:

  • keeping relationships with pupils strictly professional
  • avoiding sharing personal information or opinions that aren’t appropriate
  • being mindful of your social media presence and how it reflects on you as a trainee teacher

Being a team player

Schools are collaborative environments. Showing that you’re willing to contribute and support others goes a long way:

  • offer help where appropriate, such as supporting clubs or school events
  • be respectful of your colleagues’ time and responsibilities
  • show appreciation for the support you receive

Handling challenges professionally

Teacher training can be intense, and you may face difficult situations. How you respond is part of your professional development:

  • stay calm and seek support when needed
  • be honest if you’re struggling, and ask for help early
  • take responsibility for mistakes and learn from them
