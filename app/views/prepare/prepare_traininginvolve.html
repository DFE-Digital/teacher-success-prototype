What your training should involve

{% include "prepare/sidebar.html" %}

All teacher training must follow the initial teacher training and early career framework (ITTECF).

This sets out the entitlement of every trainee and early career teacher (ECT), what you need to know and know how to do at the start of your career.

Training providers are expected to translate this framework into their own curriculum for you to follow. The ITTECF is a minimum entitlement and not a full curriculum or an assessment framework.

Course structure

Your training should include at least:

  • 2 placements in contrasting schools
  • 120 total days in school placement
  • 15 hours on average each week in classrooms (including observing, teaching and co-teaching)
  • 6 weeks (30 days) spent teaching 80% of a full teaching timetable

What your training should include

Mentoring

You should receive clear, consistent and effective mentoring through structured feedback from expert colleagues.

This should use the best available evidence to provide a structured process for improving practice.

You must have access to mentors who have expertise in your subject or phase-specific approaches set out in your curriculum.

You should receive 1.5 hours per week (per 5 days) of mentoring support.

Receiving feedback

Your teaching should be observed in order for you to get feedback. This should be on applying a particular approach or approaches to understand what makes it successful or unsuccessful.

You should work with expert colleagues to identify areas for and ways of improving practice, seeking challenge and critique from expert colleagues, taking the lead in your own development.

Opportunities to practise, including through school placements

Providers should ensure that you have multiple opportunities to rehearse and refine particular approaches throughout your training. This could include beginning outside the classroom before using approaches in the classroom.

Safeguarding

Providers should ensure that you are fully aware of your responsibilities in respect of safeguarding and equalities legislation.

Intensive training and practise

As part of your training, you should experience a minimum of 4 weeks Intensive Training and Practice (ITaP). This is separate from your school placements and will be on top of your 120 days experience.

The ITaP is to enable you to have specific and focused time practicing parts of the teacher training curriculum in an intensive setting. You’ll get immediate and targeted feedback.

Course content

All teacher training should work towards helping you meet the teachers’ standards. This is what you’ll be assessed against at the end of your training.

These standards include behaviour management, pedagogy, curriculum, assessment and professional behaviours.
