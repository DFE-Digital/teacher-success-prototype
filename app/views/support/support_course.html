Support through your training course

Find out what support you'll get as part of your teacher training course.

{% include "support/sidebar.html" %}

There are lots of people on hand to support you during your training. These might be called different things depending on your training provider, but you can expect to receive support from the following places.

Your school-based mentor

Your school-based mentor

Your school-based mentor is there to support you with your day-to-day learning and development within your placement school. They can help you with:

  • lesson planning
  • classroom management
  • support dealing with teaching challenges, for example, pupil behaviour, using school systems or workload

Your provider-based mentor or tutor

Your provider-based mentor or tutor

Your provider-based mentor or tutor is your point of contact for your specific training course. They can help you with:

  • questions about your training course, for example, how you should be gathering evidence for qualified teacher status (QTS)
  • assessment questions
  • liaising between you and your placement school, for example, if you do not feel youâ€™re being adequately supported

Your training provider

Your training provider

Your training provider may offer services beyond your day to day placement experiences and learning. They can help you with:

  • questions or concerns with the content of your training programme
  • access to specific resources or materials
  • additional support, for example, your provider might offer careers, mental health or financial services
